# Knowledge-to-Agent Skill Binding

Version: 0.2.0

## 1. Purpose

The Knowledge-to-Agent Skill Binding records how approved enterprise knowledge is transformed into an AI-agent skill.

The binding preserves the connection between:

* the original enterprise knowledge record;
* the resulting agent skill;
* the transformation process;
* information retained or lost during transformation;
* inherited permissions and restrictions;
* validation evidence;
* deployment conditions;
* human-review requirements.

The binding exists to prevent an AI-agent skill from becoming detached from the knowledge, people, evidence, and governance boundaries that made it possible.

## 2. Core principle

> A skill must not outlive or outrun its origin.

An AI-agent skill must remain bound to the status, permissions, scope, attribution requirements, and evidence limitations of its originating knowledge.

A skill must not become more permissive merely because the original knowledge has been converted into machine-executable form.

## 3. Binding model

```text
Enterprise Knowledge Origin Record
                │
                ▼
       Transformation Process
                │
                ├── retained knowledge
                ├── lost context
                ├── distortion risks
                └── human review
                │
                ▼
          Agent Skill Definition
                │
                ├── inputs
                ├── outputs
                ├── invocation conditions
                ├── prohibited behavior
                └── required tools
                │
                ▼
         Policy and Validation
                │
                ▼
       Controlled Agent Deployment
```

## 4. Normative requirements

A conforming Knowledge-to-Agent Skill Binding MUST:

1. reference an approved Enterprise Knowledge Origin Record;
2. verify the integrity digest of the referenced origin;
3. identify the resulting agent skill and its version;
4. define the skill's inputs and outputs;
5. state the conditions under which the skill may be invoked;
6. state prohibited agent behavior;
7. record how the knowledge was transformed;
8. identify human, agent, team, or system participants in the transformation;
9. state which elements of the origin were retained;
10. record known information loss or distortion;
11. preserve origin attribution;
12. inherit the permissions of the origin record;
13. state allowed and prohibited actions;
14. define human escalation conditions;
15. include validation test cases;
16. define deployment and rollback conditions;
17. preserve an explicit lifecycle and review status.

A conforming implementation MUST NOT:

1. bind a skill to a suspended or retired origin;
2. weaken origin permissions without a separate approved governance process;
3. remove mandatory origin attribution;
4. authorize actions prohibited by the origin record;
5. deploy an untested skill into production as active;
6. continue using a skill after its origin becomes invalid;
7. conceal known transformation loss or distortion.

## 5. Transformation record

The transformation section describes the path from human-readable or system-held knowledge to machine-usable skill logic.

Transformation methods may include:

* manual encoding;
* agent-assisted encoding;
* rule extraction;
* prompt compilation;
* workflow compilation;
* hybrid transformation.

Every transformation step identifies:

* what action was performed;
* who or what performed it;
* what result was produced;
* whether additional review was required.

## 6. Loss and distortion

Enterprise knowledge cannot always be converted into structured logic without loss.

Examples include:

* sensory judgment not represented in available data;
* missing historical context;
* local terminology flattened into generic labels;
* exceptions omitted during rule extraction;
* human uncertainty converted into false precision.

Such losses are not implementation footnotes.

They are part of the formal binding and must be accompanied by mitigation controls.

## 7. Permission inheritance

The field `inherit_origin_permissions` must be `true`.

This means the skill inherits:

* confidentiality restrictions;
* permitted uses;
* prohibited uses;
* attribution requirements;
* human-review requirements;
* system boundaries;
* retention obligations.

A downstream skill may be more restrictive than its origin.

It must not become less restrictive through transformation alone.

## 8. Validation

Validation must test more than successful execution.

It should include:

* normal cases;
* edge cases;
* missing-data cases;
* conflicting-evidence cases;
* out-of-scope invocations;
* prohibited-action attempts;
* origin-trace preservation;
* human-escalation behavior.

Validation results must be linked to evidence.

## 9. Deployment

Deployment status is one of:

* `not_deployed`
* `pilot`
* `active`
* `suspended`
* `retired`

Production deployment does not necessarily mean autonomous operation.

A production skill may remain advisory and require human approval for every consequential action.

## 10. Rollback relationship

A skill must be suspended or rolled back when:

* the origin record is suspended or retired;
* origin evidence becomes invalid;
* operational scope changes;
* validation performance regresses;
* attribution disappears;
* permissions are violated;
* safety thresholds are exceeded.

The origin record therefore acts as a live dependency rather than a historical citation.

## 11. Relationship to future versions

Version 0.2 establishes the Derivative layer.

Future versions may record:

* cross-department agent executions;
* decisions and actions taken through the skill;
* human corrections and overrides;
* downstream organizational value;
* contribution and value-return allocation.

The expected lineage is:

```text
v0.1 Enterprise Knowledge Origin Record
  ↓
v0.2 Knowledge-to-Agent Skill Binding
  ↓
v0.3 Cross-Department Execution Trace
  ↓
v0.4 Human Contribution Attribution
  ↓
v0.5 Enterprise Value Return Ledger
```

## 12. Non-goals

Version 0.2 does not:

* determine legal ownership;
* calculate monetary compensation;
* prove the universal correctness of a skill;
* authorize unrestricted autonomy;
* replace model or software security testing;
* replace operational engineering review;
* record complete execution histories;
* calculate downstream business value.

Version 0.2 answers one essential question:

> How did this enterprise knowledge become this agent skill, and which boundaries must survive the transformation?
