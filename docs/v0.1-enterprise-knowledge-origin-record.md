# Enterprise Knowledge Origin Record

Version: 0.1.0

## 1. Purpose

The Enterprise Knowledge Origin Record preserves the origin of enterprise
knowledge before that knowledge is transformed into an AI-agent skill,
workflow, policy, prompt, model input, or automated decision rule.

The record exists to prevent enterprise knowledge from becoming detached from:

- its original contributors;
- its operational context;
- its supporting evidence;
- its known limitations;
- its usage permissions;
- its human-review requirements;
- its governance status.

## 2. Core principle

> Origin must be recorded before automation begins.

An AI agent must not treat captured knowledge as context-free organizational
property when that knowledge was created through identifiable human,
technical, legal, or operational contributions.

## 3. What counts as an origin

An origin may include:

- human expertise;
- team practice;
- process documentation;
- system records;
- regulatory requirements;
- customer feedback;
- external standards;
- research findings;
- combinations of multiple sources.

The origin record does not claim that every item has a single creator.

Instead, it records the contribution structure that produced the usable
knowledge.

## 4. Normative requirements

A conforming Enterprise Knowledge Origin Record MUST:

1. contain a unique record identifier;
2. identify the enterprise and operational context;
3. state the knowledge origin type;
4. describe the knowledge before agent transformation;
5. record known contributors where permitted;
6. preserve contributor attribution preferences;
7. record how the knowledge was captured;
8. include at least one evidence reference;
9. state operational limitations;
10. state permitted and prohibited uses;
11. indicate whether human review is required;
12. expose governance and safety flags;
13. include an explicit lifecycle and review status.

A conforming implementation SHOULD:

1. use pseudonymous identifiers when personal identity is unnecessary;
2. separate contributor identity from normal agent-visible content;
3. cryptographically bind records to their source material;
4. support suspension and retirement;
5. preserve records after operational use ends;
6. prevent downstream systems from removing attribution requirements.

## 5. Record lifecycle

The lifecycle status is one of:

- `draft`
- `pending_review`
- `approved`
- `suspended`
- `retired`

### Draft

The origin has been captured but is not approved for operational use.

### Pending review

The record is undergoing technical, legal, operational, or ethical review.

### Approved

The knowledge may be used within the declared permissions and system boundary.

### Suspended

Use is temporarily blocked because evidence, context, safety, or permissions
have changed.

### Retired

The knowledge is no longer approved for new use, but the record remains
available for audit, attribution, and historical traceability.

## 6. Relationship to downstream versions

Version 0.1 records the knowledge origin only.

Future protocol layers may bind this record to:

- AI-agent skills;
- cross-department executions;
- human contribution calculations;
- value-attribution records;
- royalty or benefit-return ledgers.

A downstream record SHOULD reference the `origin_id` and `record_id` rather
than copying the knowledge without provenance.

## 7. Non-goals

Version 0.1 does not:

- calculate monetary compensation;
- determine legal ownership;
- assign royalty percentages;
- authorize autonomous execution;
- prove that captured knowledge is universally correct;
- replace enterprise security or privacy controls;
- replace human engineering or compliance review.

## 8. Minimum viable interpretation

A minimal implementation may store the records as YAML or JSON documents.

A production implementation may use:

- an append-only ledger;
- signed provenance records;
- access-controlled knowledge graphs;
- enterprise policy engines;
- agent execution logs;
- royalty or benefit-allocation systems.

The storage mechanism may vary.

The origin and attribution chain must not disappear.
