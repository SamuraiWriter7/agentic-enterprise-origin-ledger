# Human Contribution Attribution

Version: 0.4.0

## 1. Purpose

The Human Contribution Attribution record identifies and evaluates the human
contributions that made an enterprise AI outcome possible.

It connects contribution claims to the existing protocol lineage:

```text
Enterprise Knowledge Origin Record
    ‚Üì
Knowledge-to-Agent Skill Binding
    ‚Üì
Cross-Department Execution Trace
    ‚Üì
Human Contribution Attribution
```

The record covers contributions made before, during, and after agent execution,
including:

- creation of the original enterprise knowledge;
- provision and review of supporting evidence;
- capture and transformation of tacit knowledge;
- domain review of an agent skill;
- contextual judgment during a live execution;
- correction or override of an agent output;
- accountable approval or rejection;
- outcome verification;
- long-term knowledge stewardship.

Version 0.4 does not assign money. It prepares an auditable contribution basis
for a future Enterprise Value Return Ledger.

## 2. Core principle

> Visibility is not the same as causality.

The person who appears at the final approval step is not automatically the sole
source of value. Likewise, the original knowledge creator is not automatically
responsible for every later operational decision.

Contribution must be attributed across the full causal lineage.

## 4. Normative requirements

A conforming Human Contribution Attribution record MUST:

1. reference the applicable v0.1 origin record;
2. reference the applicable v0.2 skill binding;
3. reference one or more v0.3 execution traces;
4. verify the integrity of every referenced execution trace;
5. declare the outcomes for which attribution is being performed;
6. declare that monetary allocation is outside the v0.4 scope;
7. publish the attribution methodology and its dimension weights;
8. require a counterfactual assessment for each human contributor;
9. connect each claimed contribution to specific evidence;
10. distinguish individuals, human teams, agents, and systems;
11. record non-human support without misrepresenting it as human contribution;
12. calculate a weighted score under a declared methodology;
13. calculate a provisional or confirmed relative weight;
14. preserve unattributed contribution when evidence is incomplete;
15. record decision authority and risk responsibility separately from creative
    or technical contribution;
16. expose open disputes and appeal rights;
17. prohibit use as an automatic employee ranking or disciplinary mechanism;
18. include independent governance review;
19. include integrity and retention controls;
20. state whether the attribution is ready for future value allocation.

## 5. Human contribution categories

Version 0.4 recognizes contribution events including:

- `origin_creation`
- `evidence_provision`
- `knowledge_capture`
- `skill_transformation`
- `domain_review`
- `operational_judgment`
- `correction`
- `approval`
- `rejection`
- `escalation`
- `coordination`
- `outcome_verification`
- `stewardship`

These categories describe what happened. They do not, by themselves, determine
the final weight.

## 6. Causal roles

A contribution may be classified as:

- `necessary` ‚Äî the scoped result would be blocked without it;
- `enabling` ‚Äî the result would be delayed or materially weakened;
- `corrective` ‚Äî it changed or repaired the execution path;
- `authorizing` ‚Äî it supplied accountable permission for consequential action;
- `validating` ‚Äî it confirmed evidence or outcome quality;
- `contextual` ‚Äî it supplied situation-specific interpretation;
- `mixed` ‚Äî multiple causal functions were present.

Every contributor must also include a counterfactual statement describing what
would likely have happened without the contribution.

## 7. Attribution dimensions

The methodology defines weighted dimensions. A deployment may choose its own
approved dimensions, but the example uses:

- origin novelty;
- domain expertise;
- contextual judgment;
- corrective action;
- authorization responsibility;
- outcome validation;
- stewardship.

Dimension weights must sum to `1.0`.

Each contributor receives a raw score from `0` to `100` for every declared
dimension. The weighted score is:

```text
weighted_score =
    Œ£(raw_dimension_score √ó declared_dimension_weight)
```

The weighted score is evidence for attribution review. It is not automatically
equal to the final relative weight.

## 8. Relative weight

`relative_weight` expresses a contributor's provisional or confirmed share of
the human contribution to the specifically declared outcomes.

It does not represent:

- the contributor's total value to the organization;
- salary entitlement;
- legal ownership;
- liability;
- intellectual-property status;
- a final royalty percentage.

Human relative weights plus `unattributed_weight` must total `1.0`.

Unattributed weight should be retained when value depends on shared
infrastructure, undocumented historical work, collective practice, or evidence
that cannot yet be assigned fairly.

## 9. Responsibility is not identical to contribution

The record separates:

```text
Contribution
    from
Decision authority
    from
Risk responsibility
```

A human approver may carry final operational responsibility without being the
originator of the knowledge.

An originator may make a large knowledge contribution without authorizing a
specific operational action.

This separation prevents both authority capture and origin erasure.

## 10. Non-human support

Agents and systems may be causally important.

Examples include:

- sensor systems;
- recommendation agents;
- policy engines;
- workflow routers;
- recordkeeping systems;
- model or tool infrastructure.

Their support must be recorded, but v0.4 excludes them from the human
attribution pool.

This prevents two opposite errors:

1. pretending that the AI did nothing;
2. pretending that an AI system is a human contributor.

A later protocol may separately recognize infrastructure or provider value.

## 11. Governance and appeal

Attribution affects recognition and may later affect value return. It therefore
requires procedural safeguards.

A conforming implementation should provide:

- access to the evidence supporting each score;
- explanation of counterfactual reasoning;
- a route to challenge omitted or duplicated contribution;
- identity protection;
- independent review;
- explicit prohibition on automated disciplinary use;
- versioned methodology;
- preservation of prior attribution records.

Open disputes should block value-return readiness.

## 12. Allocation readiness

`allocation_readiness` may be:

- `not_ready`
- `provisional`
- `ready`
- `disputed`

A record should become `ready` only when:

- business effects have adequate verification;
- relative weights and unattributed weight total `1.0`;
- evidence coverage is sufficient;
- no material dispute remains open;
- permissions and identity protections have been confirmed;
- governance review has accepted the record.

Only a `ready` attribution record should be used as an input to v0.5.

## 13. Relationship to v0.5

Version 0.5 may bind:

```text

        +
Approved v0.4 attribution

Allocation policy
        +
Eligibility and reserve rules
        =

```

Version 0.4 supplies attribution, not payment.

## 14. Non-goals

Version 0.4 does not:

- assign monetary value;
- transfer money;
- determine employment compensation;
- determine legal ownership;
- determine legal liability;
- replace collective bargaining or employment law;
- rank employees generally;
- reward only the most visible final actor;
- erase shared or unattributed contribution;
- treat an AI-generated score as self-validating.

Version 0.4 answers:

> Which documented human contributions causally supported this scoped
> enterprise outcome, and how should their relative attribution be represented
> before any value-return decision is made?
